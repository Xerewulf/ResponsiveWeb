Climate

Ankara has a continental climate with hot, dry summers and cold winters. Summers are generally hot and dry, with temperatures reaching around 30-35 degrees Celsius (86-95 degrees Fahrenheit). Winters can be cold, with temperatures often dropping below freezing, and occasional snowfall.

Location

Ankara is situated in the region known as Central Anatolia. It is surrounded by the provinces of Kırıkkale, Aksaray, Eskişehir, Bolu, Çankırı, and Konya. The city is located on a high plateau, approximately 938 meters (3,077 feet) above sea level.

History

Ankara has a rich history that dates back to ancient times. It has been inhabited by various civilizations, including the Hittites, Phrygians, Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans. In 1923, after the establishment of the Republic of Turkey, Ankara was declared as the capital by the country's founder, Mustafa Kemal Atatürk.
